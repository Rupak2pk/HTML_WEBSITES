Introduction to Generation 1

Pokémon is a series of video games developed by Game Freak and published by Nintendo and The Pokémon Company under the Pokémon media franchise. Created by Satoshi Tajiri with assistance from Ken Sugimori, the first games, Pocket Monsters Red and Green, released in 1996 in Japan for the Game Boy, and the main series of role-playing video games (RPGs), referred as the "core series" by their developers, have continued on each generation of Nintendo's handhelds.

The name Pokémon was coined for the US version of the games, which were released a few years later. There are 151 official Pokemon in the Generation 1 games.

An interesting fact is that certain pokemon only appear in certain color versions, which meant that to catch all 151, you would need to find someone playing a different color, and trade some of your extra pokemon for ones you didn't hane.

Additionally, as you played, your pokemon would gain in power and "evolve" into more powerful forms. Some pokemon though, couldn't evovle into their final forms without first being traded at least once. This naturally didn't hurt sales of the game, and also made it much more social as players networked to help each other "Catch 'em All."

Pokémon 152

Pokémon 152 is the result of a game programming glitch, that resulted from an extremely specific series of moves that resulted in the game addressing an incorrect memory location for a pokemon. the resulting graphic, which is pictured below, resembles a misshapen QR code block.

Players could then capture this "Pokémon" and add it to their collection, bringing their total to 152.
